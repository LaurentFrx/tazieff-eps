## Glossary

All strength training technical terms, explained simply. Click on a term to jump to it directly.

---

### <span id="rm">RM — Maximum Repetition</span>

The maximum load you can lift for a specific number of repetitions with correct technique. The **1RM** is the maximum load for a single repetition.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/rm-rir-rpe)

---

### <span id="rir">RIR — Reps In Reserve</span>

The number of repetitions you could still perform before reaching muscle failure. An **RIR 2** means you could have done 2 more repetitions.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/rm-rir-rpe)

---

### <span id="rpe">RPE — Rating of Perceived Exertion</span>

A subjective scale from 1 to 10 that rates the overall difficulty felt during a set. An **RPE 8** corresponds to a hard set with about 2 reps in reserve.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/rm-rir-rpe)

---

### <span id="rep">Repetition (Rep)</span>

One complete movement of an exercise, from the starting position back to that position. Example: one lift + one lowering on biceps curl = 1 repetition.

---

### <span id="serie">Set</span>

A group of repetitions performed without rest. Example: 3 sets of 10 repetitions = 3 × 10 reps with recovery time between each set.

---

### <span id="intensite">Intensity</span>

The effort level relative to your maximum capacity. Expressed as a percentage of 1RM (e.g., 70% of 1RM) or via RPE/RIR.

---

### <span id="volume">Volume</span>

The total amount of work in a session or a week. Calculated as: **sets × reps × load**. Progressive volume is key to progress.

---

### <span id="recuperation">Recovery</span>

The rest time between two sets or two sessions. Varies by goal: 30s to 1 min 30s (endurance), 1 min 30s to 3 min (hypertrophy), 3 to 5 min (strength/power).

---

### <span id="agoniste">Agonist</span>

The primary muscle that produces the movement by contracting. Example: the **biceps** is the agonist during elbow flexion.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/muscles)

---

### <span id="antagoniste">Antagonist</span>

The muscle opposite to the agonist, which stretches to allow and control the movement. Example: the **triceps** is the antagonist during elbow flexion.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/muscles)

---

### <span id="isolation">Isolation Exercise</span>

An exercise targeting **a single muscle** through **a single joint**. Examples: biceps curl, leg extension, dumbbell fly.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/muscles)

---

### <span id="polyarticulaire">Compound Exercise</span>

An exercise engaging **multiple muscles** through **multiple joints**. Examples: squat, bench press, pull-ups, rows. Place at the beginning of a session.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/muscles)

---

### <span id="concentrique">Concentric Contraction</span>

The muscle shortens to produce the movement. This is the push or pull phase. Example: the upward movement of a biceps curl.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/contractions)

---

### <span id="isometrique">Isometric Contraction</span>

The muscle produces force without changing length — it holds a position. Example: holding the bottom of a squat, plank.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/contractions)

---

### <span id="excentrique">Eccentric Contraction</span>

The muscle lengthens under tension to slow the movement down. This is the controlled lowering phase. The most effective for muscle development.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/contractions)

---

### <span id="pliometrique">Plyometric Contraction</span>

A rapid sequence of a stretch (eccentric) followed by an explosive contraction (concentric). The spring principle. Example: squat jump.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/contractions)

---

### <span id="stabilisateurs">Core Stabilizers</span>

Muscles that maintain the spine in a neutral position during effort: abdominals (front) and quadratus lumborum / erector spinae (back). Essential for safety.

→ [Learn more](/apprendre/securite)

---

### <span id="endurance-de-force">Strength Endurance</span>

The ability to sustain muscular effort over time. Trained with light to moderate loads (50–70% of 1RM) and long sets (12–20 reps).

---

### <span id="hypertrophie">Hypertrophy</span>

Increase in muscle size. Trained with moderate loads (65–80% of 1RM), sets of 8–12 reps, and sufficient volume.

---

### <span id="puissance">Power</span>

The ability to produce maximum force in minimum time. Trained with heavy loads (80–95% of 1RM), short sets (1–6 reps), and complete recovery.
