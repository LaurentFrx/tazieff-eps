## Why Follow a Program?

A weekly program structures your training over time: it distributes muscle groups, ensures recovery, and avoids imbalances. These 3 examples by Frederique Proisy are starting points — adapt them to your resources and goals.

> These programs are **reference examples**, not strict prescriptions. The goal is to understand the construction logic so you can create your own.

---

## Program 1 — Strength and Toning

**Goal:** Hypertrophy and strength endurance — shape the lower body, tone the back, strengthen the core.

**Intensity:** Moderate — muscle fatigue on the last 2 reps, 1 min to 1 min 30s recovery.

**Method:** Constant load or pyramid on main exercises.

---

### Session A — Lower Body and Glutes (Shaping)

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|----------|------|------|-------|
| Squat | 3 | 10–12 | Main compound exercise |
| Hip Thrust | 3 | 10–12 | 1 sec pause at the top |
| Lunges | 3 | 10 | Alternating or walking |
| Deadlift | 3 | 10 | Flat back, active core bracing |
| Front Plank | 3 | 30s | Static core bracing |

---

### Session B — Upper Body and Posture

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|----------|------|------|-------|
| Seated Row | 3 | 10–12 | Lats |
| Shoulder Press | 3 | 10 | Dumbbells or barbell |
| Lat Pulldown | 3 | 10 | Lats + biceps |
| Push-ups | 2–3 | 8–10 | Full range of motion |
| Biceps Curls | 2 | 12 | Isolation finisher |
| Side Plank | 3 | 30s | Each side |

---

### Session C — Lower Body + Abs

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Notes |
|----------|------|------|-------|
| Goblet Squat | 3 | 12 | Dumbbell in front, upright torso |
| Step Up | 3 | 10 | Each leg |
| Hip Abduction | 3 | 15 | Machine or band |
| Leg Curl | 3 | 12 | Hamstrings |
| Transverse Abs | 4 | 10s | Contract + release |
| Dynamic Plank | 3 | 30s | Mountain climbers |

## Program 2 — Fat Loss and Muscle Definition

**Goal:** Maximum energy expenditure + muscle mass maintenance.

**Intensity:** High — you should finish out of breath but still able to talk (ideal fat burning zone).

**Mandatory warm-up before each session:**
→ 2 rounds of: 20 Jumping Jacks + 10 Squats + 10 Shoulder Rotations + 10 Lunges + 20s Plank

---

### Session 1 — Lower Body & Cardio (45 min)

**Block A — 4 rounds:**
- Goblet Squat × 12
- Alternating Lunges × 12/leg
- Hip Thrust × 15

**Block B — 4 rounds:**
- Kettlebell Swings × 20 *(massive energy expenditure)*
- Mountain Climbers × 30s
- Plank × 30s
- Jump Rope × 30s

---

### Session 2 — Upper Body & Core (50 min)

**Block A — 4 rounds:**
- Push-ups × 15
- Kettlebell Press × 12
- Dips × 12

**Block B — 4 rounds:**
- Rows × 12/arm
- Curls × 12
- Side Plank × 30s

---

### Session 3 — Full Body Fat Burner (45 min)

**Intense circuit of 5 rounds:**
- Swings × 15
- Squat + Thruster × 12
- Rows × 12/arm
- Push-ups × 12
- Russian Twist × 20

> Ideal zone: out of breath but able to talk. If you can't talk, slow down. If you can sing, speed up.

---

### Session 4 — Special Fat Loss Option (20 min)

**30/30 format (effort/rest) on repeat:**
- Swings
- Jumping Jacks
- Squats
- Mountain Climbers
- Plank

*Ideal as a supplement or on days when time is short.*

## Program 3 — Strength Training and Calisthenics

**Goal:** Develop relative strength (strength relative to bodyweight) and master calisthenics skills.

**Core principles:**
- **Relative strength & control**: get stronger at bodyweight, improve coordination
- **Specific execution**: slow, controlled movements, focused on strength or isometrics

---

### Pillar 1 — Mechanical Tension (Raw Strength)

> Increase raw strength transferable to skills through sets of 3–8 reps.

Reference exercises:
- Strict Pull-ups (pronated / supinated)
- Weighted Dips
- Decline Push-ups
- Pistol Squats (single leg)
- Nordic Hamstring Curl

**Parameters:** 4–5 sets × 3–8 reps, maximum intensity, 3–4 min recovery.

---

### Pillar 2 — Isometrics (Stability and Active Core Bracing)

> Key to skills for gaining stability, strengthening tendons, and active core bracing.

Reference exercises:
- Hollow Body × 10–30s
- Planche Lean × 10–30s
- Front Lever Tuck × 10–30s
- Handstand Hold × 10–30s
- L-Sit × 10–30s

**Parameters:** 3–5 sets × 10–30s depending on level, 2–3 min recovery.

---

### Pillar 3 — Eccentrics (Progress Accelerator)

> Develop strength quickly and protect against injuries.

Reference exercises:
- Negative Pull-ups × 5–10s descent
- Deep Dips (slow 4s descent)
- Controlled Handstand Push-up Descent
- Negative Front Lever

**Parameters:** 3–4 sets × 3–5 reps with 4–6s descent, 3 min recovery.

## How to Build Your Own Program

From these examples, here's the logic to remember:

**1. Choose your goal** → endurance/toning, fat loss, or strength/calisthenics

**2. Distribute sessions** → 3 sessions/week minimum, don't work the same muscle groups two days in a row

**3. Structure each session** → compound exercises first, isolation to finish

**4. Apply a method** → constant load for beginners, then pyramid, drop set, etc.

**5. Log your performance** → training log is mandatory to progress
