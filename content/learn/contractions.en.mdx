## Why Vary Contraction Types?

Varying contraction types **increases the benefits of your training** and maintains motivation throughout the cycle. Each type of contraction engages the muscle differently and produces specific adaptations.

---

## The 4 Types of Contraction

### 1. Concentric
> **The muscle shortens — the insertion points move closer together.**

This is the most common contraction in strength training. It corresponds to the **push or pull phase** of a movement.

- Example in biceps curl: the **upward** movement of the bar (the biceps shortens)
- Example in squat: the **ascent** (the quadriceps shortens)
- Sensation: main effort, muscle burning zone

---

### 2. Eccentric
> **The muscle lengthens under tension — it slows the movement down.**

This is the **controlled lowering phase**. The muscle resists the load to slow the movement. Often neglected, it is actually the most effective phase for muscle development.

- Example in biceps curl: the **slow lowering** of the bar (the biceps lengthens while braking)
- Example in squat: the **descent** (the quadriceps lengthens while controlling)
- Tip: **slow down the eccentric phase** (2 to 4 seconds) to maximize gains

---

### 3. Isometric
> **The insertion points are fixed — the muscle produces force without moving.**

The muscle develops maximum tension **without changing length**. Useful for strengthening a specific joint angle or for core bracing.

- Example: holding the bottom position of a squat (2–3 second pause)
- Example: plank in push-up position
- Example: holding a load midway through a curl
- Method application: **Stato-Dynamic** → [see the method](/methodes/stato-dynamique)

---

### 4. Plyometric
> **Combines a rapid eccentric phase followed by an explosive concentric phase.**

The muscle stores elastic energy during the eccentric phase (rapid stretch) and immediately releases it in an explosive contraction (shortening). This is the spring principle.

- Example: squat jump (rapid descent → explosive rebound)
- Example: plyometric push-ups (descent → explosive push with hands leaving the ground)
- Goal: develop **explosive power**
- Method application: **Plyometrics** → [see the method](/methodes/pliometrie)

## Summary Table

| Type | Muscle Movement | Typical Phase | Main Goal |
|------|----------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Concentric** | Shortens | Push / pull | Strength, hypertrophy |
| **Eccentric** | Lengthens under tension | Controlled lowering | Hypertrophy, strength |
| **Isometric** | No movement | Hold, bracing | Static strength, stability |
| **Plyometric** | Stretch → rapid shortening | Rebound, jump | Explosive power |

---

## How to Integrate Them Into Your Training

**Year 10:** first master concentric and controlled eccentric (2–3 second descent) on all your exercises.

**Year 11:** incorporate short isometric phases (pause at the bottom of the movement) to strengthen weak points.

**Year 12:** work on plyometrics to develop explosive power, following strict safety rules (complete warm-up, soft surface, full recovery).

> **Universal rule:** regardless of the contraction phase, **technique always takes priority over load**.
